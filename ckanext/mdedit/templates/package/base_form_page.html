{{ _('What are datasets?') }}

{% trans %} A Dataset is a collection of data resources (such as files), together with a description and other information ("Metadata"), at a fixed URL. Datasets are what users see when searching for data. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('What are Metadata?') }}

{% trans %} For automated searchability and exchange ("Harvesting") various formats of Metadata have been defined, the most important ones of which are the international ISO19115 format and the European Inspire format. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Which formats (Profiles) does the CCCA support?') }}

{% trans %} For your convenience the CCCA supports so far both important Metadata Formats, ISO19115 and Inspire (further profiles will follow). Below every Metadata field on the right you will find information about the corresponding ISO and Inspire fields including obligation information (mandatory, optional, conditional).
Right to each field on the right you find an informal explanation of its meaning and scope. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Do I have to provide all information?') }}

{% trans %} For your convenience the CCCA supports as well a minmal amount of information about your resources in order to upload and store them on the CCCA server (corresponding fields are marked *). However, we like to point out that the standard conformity of the information you provide regarding your data ensures worldwide detectability and linkage. {% endtrans %}
